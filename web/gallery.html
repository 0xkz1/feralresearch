Author notes

The human layer is part of the archive.

Each study needs a short note about what the artist is deciding: framing, species ambiguity, disaster context, evidence level, and what should not be overclaimed.

Do not anthropomorphize the subject.

The work is about ecological displacement and survival pressure, not sentimental mascot imagery.

Leave uncertainty visible.

When source data is incomplete, the image should show ambiguity instead of pretending the event is fully known.

Use generated images as material.

Generated outputs enter the studio process as studies, prompts, and references for authored painting and writing.

Position the animal at a distance.

Close-up portraits suggest domestication. Feral subjects read better when placed in landscape, seen through context rather than stared at.

Show the infrastructure that displaced them.

Every feral animal exists because of a human decision: war, evacuation, dam construction, mine expansion. The image should carry that origin.
